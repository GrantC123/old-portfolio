Centralizing Bankrate's historical proprietary data into one Data Center

Project Summary

As Senior Product Designer, I led the creation of Bankrate's centralized Data Center—a new hub designed to make proprietary rate indices and survey data accessible and engaging for external audiences. The goal of this project is to support journalists, content creators, and consumers with interactive, visually compelling charts that would drive backlinks and boost Bankrate's authority in the finance space.

Problem statement

Prior to this project, Bankrate's valuable financial data was scattered across multiple survey pages and formats, making it difficult for journalists and content creators to discover, access, and cite authoritative information. This fragmentation limited our visibility in the media and reduced opportunities for earning backlinks, ultimately impacting Bankrate's search ranking and authority.

Research

We first started out doing team co-creation. We participated in a full hour co-creation session. Here are the artifacts from that session. We aligned that we wanted to have a search bar, use highchart, and have a hub and chart pages. We first looked around at other similar websites and competitors and grabbed screenshots then left sticky notes.

I conducted my own competitive analysis to familiarize myself with the data industry landscape. During my research, I observed several common features across many sites, including:

  • A search bar
  • A hub page
  • Category pages
  • Tagging systems
  • A dedicated chart page
  • Multiple navigation options to access the chart page

My role and contribution

I managed the full design process—from synthesizing business goals and user research, to facilitating cross-functional workshops and iterating on wireframes and prototypes in Figma. I collaborated closely with engineers to ensure feasibility, contributed to the design system, and presented progress and rationale to senior stakeholders, incorporating their feedback to refine the final solution.
